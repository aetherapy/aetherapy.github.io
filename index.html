About me

I am Agathe, a licensed therapist, and member of the NIP association, based in Utrecht. I have master’s degrees in Clinical Psychology and Social and Health Psychology from the University of Utrecht. I have experience working with individuals and organizations.

I offer individual therapy to adults (18 years+) in French and English. Sessions can take place in person or online.

About the therapy

Through the psychotherapy process, we explore our feelings and behaviors to gain valuable insight into ourselves. With better understanding, we get more agency over our lives and can create a unique environment that suits us. We either remove or learn how to handle stressors and obstacles, freeing the path to reaching our goals.

I help clients working through:

  • Anxiety including panic attacks
  • Depressive moods, low energy, and problems with concentration
  • Unhappiness and emptiness
  • Traumas and aversive events from childhood
  • Interpersonal issues, complex family dynamics
  • Irritability, difficulties with setting boundaries
  • Self-esteem issues, lack of confidence, difficulty reaching goals
  • Sleeping problems such as insomnia and nightmares
  • Problems related to living abroad e.g. homesickness, loneliness, cultural differences
Learn more about the therapy
  • Therapy can be beneficial for anyone experiencing emotional distress, mental health issues, or seeking personal growth. A consultation can help determine the best course of action.
  • The first 30-minute meeting is free and allows us to get to know each other. If we both feel a connection and I believe I can assist you, we can schedule the next session and begin the therapy process.
  • The frequency of sessions depends on your individual needs and therapeutic goals. Typically, clients meet weekly or bi-weekly.
  • The standard price of a 1 hour session is 90 euros. For pacients struggling with financial problems I offer a discounted rate. Unfortunately the sessions are not covered by insurance.
  • The length of therapy varies. Some people feel better after a few sessions, while others may benefit from longer-term support.
  • If you are experiencing a mental health crisis, please contact emergency services or go to the nearest emergency room. We provide crisis support during business hours but are not a 24/7 crisis center.
